Winning with Losers

The Gist

Total bets: $1,409.53

Winnings: $154.98

The Seattle Mariners won me $89.98.

I occasionally, perhaps more than just occasionally, think about various betting strategies and what would yield the most money per bet. People who like to gamble love to discuss who is going to win, but very little attention is paid to the teams that lose. So I started making fictitious bets against the three teams with the most losses in the MLB after the All-Star break. The dubious honors go to the Baltimore Orioles, Chicago White Sox and Kansas City Royals.

As it turned out, the Orioles and the Royals lost more times than the top two teams won. Boston won 108 times and Houston won 103 times, while the Orioles and Royals lost 115 and 104 respectively. The White Sox lost the same amount of times as the Yankees at 100.

I started betting on the White Sox on June 18th, followed by the Orioles on June 19th, and the Royals on June 29th. I missed some games because if you place a bet on the listed pitchers and if either teams end up starting someone else then the bet is cancelled. Also, a few times there wasn't an available line when I was making the wagers so I missed those bets as well.

So we're clear, I don't think anyone should bet on anything ever.
