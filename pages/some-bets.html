Betting on the NFL

Every so often I think that I can make money while gambling on the NFL, so now I'm going to see if that's true or if I would have been better off just putting my money into my savings account. I'm going to record all of my bets here and you can follow along to see how things go.

I do realize that there are a lot of major corporations who make a ton of money on people betting on the NFL. I'm sure they use huge databases and measure a ton of variables that I don't know about, so that should be interesting.

All of the bets below are moneyline bets, so they don't include points. For example, the Patriots over the Dolphins would be a moneyline bet. I have made some over/under bets, which is where there's a set number of points that the teams will combine to score, like 48.5 and you bet on whether the combined score will be under or over 48.5. Oddsmakers use the half point so that there isn't a chance of a tie.

Here's how the results below are calculated. If I bet $10 on the Cowboys to beat the Giants, and the payout is $5 because the Cowboys are favored, if the Cowboys win the the Cowboys would add $5 in the Results from teams I bet on to win column and the Giants would add $5 in the Results from teams I bet against column.

If the Cowboys were to lose against the Giants, the Cowboys would have -$10 in the Results from teams I bet on to win column and the Giants would have -$10 in the Results from teams I bet against column.
